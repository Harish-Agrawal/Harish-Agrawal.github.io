Objective

Our primary objective was to build a cost-effective, light-weight and reliable Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) capable of carrying a payload. Each of the components have been chosen with rigorous survey in order to provide maximum throughput with minimal cost. Weight is a crucial factor when building any aerial vehicle. It can be a deciding factor for the overall cost since it determines the specifications of the power system. Utmost care was taken to ensure stability, by providing a dedicated floating-point processor for precise PID calculations.

The secondary objective was to make it full-fledged, laden with the latest technology available in the market and finally making it capable of autonomous flights.

Implemeted features like:

  • Return to home.
  • Altitude/position hold.
  • Constant monitoring of flight conditions using Telemetry.
  • Live video streaming for surveillance.
  • Mission planning – setting waypoints.

We are trying to forefront the upcoming field of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) so as to provide a benchmark for further development in this area.

Conclusions

  • We first tested and evaluated all the software related work on a small quadcopter which cost around 7-9k rs.
  • We were able to procure and assemble various components to build Nimble.
  • Performed course PID(Proportional Integral Derivative) tuning indoor and then did fine tuning by flying outdoor.
  • Analyzed black-box data for any discripensies due to vibrational noise. This was crucial so as to know exactly how Nimble is flying in terms of power efficiency and component handling.
  • From the black-box analysis, we understood that due to high-frequency noise Flight Controller(FC) was trying hard to keep Nimble at one place forcing ESC to switch rapidly thereby causing motors to heat significantly. Applied digital filtering, soft mounting motors as well as FC to reduce the noise reaching FC.
  • We were able to build Nimble in 30-35k rs.
